Ethics behaviours

The definition of ethical behavior or ethics as a whole is one that eludes many people. There have been many philosophers that have tried to create a set of guidelines that create a code or baseline to a decision. Immanuel Kant is one such person who has created some of the bases that all theories have been based. Kant’s principals or the categorical imperative is the base for the “Golden Rule”; which is taught to young children. Do on to others, as you would have others do on to you. To quote Kant, “Act that your principle of action might safely be made a law for the whole world.” Ethical behavior is the way that a person should act using a set of rules or standards that have been formed within a community of people.
